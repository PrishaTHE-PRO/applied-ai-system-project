# Feeding Guidelines for Dogs and Cats

## Frequency
Adult dogs typically eat twice per day. Puppies under six months should eat
three to four times daily. Adult cats can be fed twice daily or have access
to measured dry food during the day.

## Portion control
Portion size depends on the pet's weight, activity level, and the calorie
density of the food. Most commercial foods print a feeding chart by weight
on the bag — use it as a starting point and adjust based on body condition.

## Foods to avoid
Never feed dogs or cats: chocolate, grapes, raisins, onions, garlic, xylitol
(common in sugar-free gum), macadamia nuts, or alcohol. Cats should not be
given raw fish regularly because it can cause thiamine deficiency.

## Water
Fresh water should always be available. A typical dog drinks roughly one ounce
of water per pound of body weight per day; cats drink less but still need
constant access.
